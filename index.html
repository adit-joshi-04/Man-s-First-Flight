Man's First Flight

A piloted machine to take off under its own power in full flight, maintained airspeed and landed at a point as high as where it started. A flight by every definition.

In 1899, The Wright Brothers were running their own bicycle shop called Wright Cycle Co.

In the same year, Wilbur wrote to the Smithsonian Institute to refer some sources to start their journey of flying.

The Wright Brother chose Kitty Hawk as their space for testing. It had big open spaces with high levels to fly off from as well as sandy grounds which was safer for crashes.
Wait... What's this?
"Never in a thousand years will man fly." -Wilbur Wright
Now to gather their own data, the brothers made the first wind tunnel in which they tested model airplanes with different wing designs. In this tunnel, they were able to test over 200 different wing designs without having to build full scale gliders.
The wind tunnel proved to be a massive success. They conducted these experiments to gather first-hand data which was more accurate than all the experimenters that came before them.
Summer of 1902, they return to Kitty Hawk eager to see the data they had collected in action.
But they still hadn't developed full control over the plane... something was still off.
But they didn't know what...
Later that night, Orville was unable to sleep. Tossing and turning he could not help but think about the plane. It was the last piece of the puzzle. What were they missing...?
"I've got it!"

"The movable rudder."
That's it! With the movable rudder the brothers had managed to gain full control over their aircraft.
The only thing left was the propulsion system.
They fit two propellers on either side of the plane giving it enough power to lift itself off the ground. They accounted for the gyroscopic stability of the plane by making the propellers rotate in opposite directions canceling out the gyroscopic movements of each other.
The only thing left to do was fly! But who would be the first one?
